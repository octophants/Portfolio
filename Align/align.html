ALIGN

Align is an interactive tablet game designed for children with Strabismus, a condition in which the eyes are not properly aligned with each other. Research shows that treatment is most effective if started at a very early stage. Align, hence, serves as a tool for children to improve their eyesight in a creative and entertaining manner. It is inspired by Alv.io.

Align tackles Strabismus in such a way that the player exercises their affected eye. The app is meant to be used along with an eyepatch that blocks the player's stronger eye. The eyepatch complements the pirate theme of the game and makes eye exercises fun and interactive for children rather than clinical and mundane. Children can play this game without feeling conscious or embarrassed of wearing an eyepatch. Moreover, they don't have to bother about practicing boring exercises at an ophthalmologist's clinic.

In the game, you are the captain of a ghost pirate ship.

You keep getting attacked by the treacherous Kraken, so you have to shoot it with cannon balls.

Here’s a mockup of the gameplay in Processing. The player has to focus on targets with their uncovered affected eye, thereby exercising and strengthening their eye muscles.

defeating the kraken results in earning 'gold'. . .

. . .which can be spent in the game’s shop to buy items.

Align's in-game economy encourages the player to earn gold by playing more so that they can purchase upgrades. The player benefits from practicing eye exercises for longer periods, since they end up wanting to play more.
